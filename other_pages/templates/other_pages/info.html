What is Cancer?

Cancer is a disease that occurs when abnormal cells in the body grow uncontrollably. These cells can form tumors or invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body. While cancer can affect people of all ages, it is more common in older adults. Abnormal growth forming a lump is called tumour, this is true of all cancers except leukaemia (cancer of the blood). If left untreated, tumours can grow and spread into the surrounding normal tissue, or to other parts of the body via the bloodstream and lymphatic systems, and can affect the digestive, nervous and circulatory systems or release hormones that may affect body function.
