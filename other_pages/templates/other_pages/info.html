What is Cancer?

Key Cancer Facts

What is Cancer?

Cancer is a disease that occurs when abnormal cells in the body grow uncontrollably. These cells can form tumors or invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body. While cancer can affect people of all ages, it is more common in older adults. Abnormal growth forming a lump is called tumour, this is true of all cancers except leukaemia (cancer of the blood). If left untreated, tumours can grow and spread into the surrounding normal tissue, or to other parts of the body via the bloodstream and lymphatic systems, and can affect the digestive, nervous and circulatory systems or release hormones that may affect body function.

Causes of cancer

Cancers can be caused by a number of different factors the most important are listed below. It is important to remember that, while some factors cannot be modified, around one third of cancer cases can be prevented by reducing behavioural and dietary risks.

Modifiable risk factors include:

  1. Alcohol
  2. Being overweight or obese
  3. Diet and nutrition
  4. Physical activity
  5. Tobacco
  6. Work place hazards
  7. Ionising radiation
  8. Infection

Non-Modifiable risk factors include:

  1. Age
  2. Cancer-causing substances
  3. Genetics
  4. The immune system

Early detection of cancer

Some ways to detect cancer early include:

  1. Regular screening tests: Depending on your age and risk factors, your doctor may recommend regular screening tests, such as mammograms, colonoscopies, or Pap tests.
  2. Know your body: Be aware of any changes in your body, such as new lumps, moles, or changes in bowel habits.
  3. Talk to your doctor: If you have any concerns or questions about your health, talk to your doctor. They can help determine if any further testing or screenings are necessary.
  4. Skin checks: Regular skin checks can help detect skin cancer early. You can perform self-examinations at home or see a dermatologist for a full-body exam.

Ways to Prevent cancer

Some ways to prevent cancer include:

  1. Maintain a healthy diet: Eating a diet that is rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein can help reduce the risk of cancer.
  2. Exercise regularly: Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight, reduce inflammation, and lower the risk of cancer.
  3. Avoid tobacco and limit alcohol consumption: Tobacco use and excessive alcohol consumption are known to increase the risk of cancer.
  4. Protect your skin: Avoid excessive sun exposure and use sunscreen and protective clothing when you are outside.
  5. Get vaccinated: Some viruses, such as HPV and hepatitis B, can increase the risk of certain cancers. Getting vaccinated can help reduce the risk.
